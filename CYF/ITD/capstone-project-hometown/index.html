Savona - Liguria, (Italy)

Priamar Fortress

The Priamar Fortress is one of Savona's most remarkable historical and cultural landmarks, perched on a hill overlooking the Ligurian Sea. Built in 1542 by the Republic of Genoa to establish dominance over Savona, the fortress has a fascinating history of conquest, rebellion and transformation. The Priamar Fortress stands as a powerful reminder of Savona’s complex past and its enduring spirit.


Tell me more!

Torre Leon Pancaldo

The Torre Leon Pancaldo, affectionately called "La Torretta" by locals, is a medieval tower that proudly stands at the entrance of Savona’s port and is one of the city’s most iconic landmarks. Built in the 14th century, the Torretta was named after Leon Pancaldo, a famous Savonese navigator and explorer who sailed with Magellan on the first circumnavigation of the globe.


Tell me more!

Sistine Chapel

The Sistine Chapel of Savona, also known as the Cappella Sistina di Savona, is one of the city’s hidden treasures and a stunning example of Italian Renaissance architecture. Located beside Savona’s Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta, it is one of only two Sistine Chapels in the world—the other, of course, being the famous one in Vatican City.


Tell me more!

Marina di Savona

The Port of Savona has been a central part of the city’s history, dating back to ancient times when it served as a bustling trade hub connecting Italy with the Mediterranean. Today, the marina has evolved to meet modern needs, featuring moorings for yachts, sailboats and smaller vessels. One of Italy’s largest commercial ports, it also retains sections dedicated to recreational boating.

Tell me more!

Torre del Brandale

The Brandale Tower is one of Savona’s iconic medieval towers, dating back to the 12th century. It once served as the main civic tower, with bells used to announce important events and warn citizens of danger. The Brandale Tower is a symbol of Savona’s medieval history and civic pride. Standing tall at the entrance to the historic center, it’s adorned with Savona’s coat of arms and the names of local nobles.

Tell me more!

Via Paleocapa

Via Paleocapa is one of Savona's most prominent streets, lined with beautiful arcaded buildings in eclectic architectural styles, dating back to the 19th century. Via Paleocapa connects the port area to the historic center and is one of the busiest and most charming parts of the city. As you stroll along Via Paleocapa, you’ll get a feel for Savona’s vibrant commercial and cultural history.
