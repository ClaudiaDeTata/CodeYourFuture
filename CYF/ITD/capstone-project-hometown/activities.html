What to do in Savona?

Relax at the Beach

Savona’s beaches are perfect for those who want to enjoy the sun, the sea and the laid-back Mediterranean vibe. Whether you prefer the lively atmosphere of private beach clubs or the more tranquil public beaches, you’ll find clear waters and soft sands where you can relax, swim or even try out water sports like kayaking and windsurfing. The beaches near Marina di Savona are especially popular and the scenery is unbeatable, with views of the rugged Ligurian coastline and the towering Priamar Fortress in the distance.

Aperitivo by the Sea

No visit to Italy would be complete without enjoying an aperitivo, the beloved pre-dinner drink and snack tradition. In Savona, you can find some fantastic spots by the waterfront to enjoy this social ritual. Picture yourself sipping an Aperol spritz or a local Ligurian wine, paired with a selection of small bites like olives, focaccia and seafood appetizers, all while watching the sunset over the harbor. The Marina di Savona is a great place for this – with its vibrant bars and cafés offering a relaxed, chic setting for an early evening aperitivo.

Stroll Through the Historic Center

Savona’s historic center is a delightful maze of narrow streets, charming piazzas and colorful buildings. Start your stroll at the Piazza Sisto IV, where you’ll find the Cathedral of the Assumption and make your way through the cobblestone streets to discover hidden gems, from local boutiques to artisan shops. Stop for a coffee or gelato in one of the charming cafés and enjoy the atmosphere of this old yet lively town. Don’t forget to check out the Monumento to the fallen in Piazza Mameli, a lovely spot perfect for people-watching or taking photos of the beautiful surroundings.

Coastal Walks to the Charming Villages

For a truly memorable experience, embark on a scenic coastal walk that takes you from Savona to the surrounding picturesque villages like Bergeggi and Finale Ligure. These walks along the Ligurian coastline offer stunning views of the turquoise sea, rugged cliffs and lush greenery. The route will lead you through peaceful nature trails, charming coves and hidden beaches. Once you reach villages like Bergeggi, known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters, or Finale Ligure, a quaint medieval town with narrow streets and local cafes, you’ll feel like you’ve discovered a hidden treasure.
