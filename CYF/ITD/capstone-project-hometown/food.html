What to eat in Savona?

Focaccia Savonese: A Slice of Tradition

In Savona, focaccia isn't just bread – it’s a warm tradition, shared and celebrated in every corner of the city. Known for its golden, crispy crust and soft, olive oil-rich center, focaccia savonese has a unique flavor that locals and visitors adore. Baked fresh each day in traditional wood-fired ovens, it’s a simple yet beloved delicacy, with recipes passed down through generations. Whether enjoyed with a sprinkle of sea salt or paired with savory toppings, focaccia captures the essence of Savona’s culinary heritage, inviting you to taste a piece of its authentic, coastal charm.

Farinata Savonese: A Taste of Coastal History

Golden, thin and slightly crispy on the edges, farinata is a beloved specialty of Savona, combining simple ingredients—chickpea flour, water and olive oil—to create a savory delight with a creamy center. Traditionally baked in large copper pans over a wood fire, it’s delicious on its own or topped with local favorites like sausage, pesto, cheese or fresh arugula. Each addition enhances farinata’s earthy flavors, making it a versatile treat perfect for every taste. Whether you enjoy it as street food or a shared dish at the table, farinata captures the warmth and culinary heart of Savona.

Trofie al Pesto: Ligurian Pasta at its Finest

A classic Ligurian dish, trofie al pesto brings together handmade, twisted pasta with the rich, fragrant flavors of fresh basil pesto. Originating in Liguria, trofie are small, rolled pasta with a shape perfect for holding pesto's creamy texture, made from crushed basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmigiano-Reggiano and extra virgin olive oil. Often served with tender green beans and potatoes, trofie al pesto is a harmonious blend of flavors, showcasing the freshness and simplicity of Ligurian cuisine. It’s a dish that transports you to the coastal heart of Savona with every bite, where tradition and taste meet in every swirl of pasta.

Panissa: Savona’s Savory Chickpea Delight

Panissa is a humble yet delicious street food staple in Savona, crafted from chickpea flour, water and a hint of salt. Similar to farinata, panissa is cooked until firm, then sliced into thick pieces and either pan-fried to a golden crisp or served cold. With a mild, nutty flavor and a creamy interior, it’s a versatile dish that’s perfect on its own or enjoyed with a sprinkle of sea salt and a drizzle of olive oil. Often paired with local wines or served alongside olives and cheeses, panissa is a true taste of Savona’s culinary heritage—simple, savory and utterly satisfying.
